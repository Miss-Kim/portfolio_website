⎡GRO⎦ responsive e-wallet mobile app

Gro is an responsive mobile payment app that allows users to shop both online and in-store (touchless), transfer, receive and split the money with others who don't use the same e-wallet app.

Overview

So, What Exactly is GRO?

GRO is a responsive e-wallet app that allows users to quickly and safely store their bank information and credit card information in one place, enabling users to shop online and in-store (touchless feature). Also, give users the freedom to split bills with others, send and request money with those who don't use the same e-wallet.

Discovery Phase

PROBLEM STATEMENT

E-wallet app needs a new way to securely store multiple cards, and bank information, whilst keeping track of all financial transactions and expenses. Also allows users to safely "transfer and request " money to others who does not use the same e-wallet app. .

SOLUTIONS

Create a universal e-wallet that can communicate with other apps, also allow users to store multiple bank information and credit cards information in one place. This way, we can offer users better control of their finance, freedom to perform online transactions with other receivers without having to use multiple e-wallet and perform touchless in-store payments.

Discovery and Research

WHO?

Age between 18 - 40, busy young professional looking for a universal e-wallet that would help save time and money while security is the greatest concern.

WHERE?

Primary use will mostly be on mobile phone, on the go.

WHEN?

Anytime a user needs an online checkout or in-store checkout. Reminder notification even when you are not using the app.

WHAT?

Empower users to be more aware of their spending habits, gain more control of their finance and offer reminders to stay on top of their budget.

WHY?

Save time, save money, never late payment fees, easy to follow instructions, touchless and financial freedom.

HOW?

Give suggestions to save up, notify when bills are soon to due, easy to follow purchased history available to users with diagrams / charts.

Problem & Solution

I explain beforehand what sketches stand for without revealing the concept to avoid bias. Since my users are not used to paper sketches, I created a mid-fidelity version of the prototypes since they are more familiar.

Information Architecture

After creating initial user research, a user flow is planned based on user needs and solutions for their problems. Based on a user flow, prototypes are drawn using pen and paper to conduct the first usability test using UsabilityHub.

Low-fidelity prototypes & Iterations

Next, I integrated user feedback and designed a mid-fidelity prototype in InVison. I then conducted further two rounds of usability tests to identify further usability issues and later analysed test results in Excel.
